Rosemary Shortbread Cookies

Description

These light and crumbly cookies have the perfect balance of rosemary flavor and slight sweetness. Great for holiday gifting, and easy to whip up anytime!

Equipment

  • Mixer
  • 2 Baking Sheets
  • Parchment Paper

Ingredients

Tip: check off each ingredient as you measure so you don't miss anything

  • 1½ C unsalted butter
  • ¾ C white sugar
  • 2 Tbsp chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2¾ C all-purpose flour
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • (optional) 2 tsp white sugar for decoration

Directions

Tip: check off each step to easily keep track of where you are

  1. Cream together butter and ⅔ C of sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Stir in the flour, salt, and rosemary until well-blended. The dough may be somewhat soft.
  3. Roll into two logs in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  4. Preheat the oven to 375Â° F. Line cookie sheets with parchment paper.
  5. Cut cookies in ¼-inch slices and place 1 inch apart on the lined cookie sheets. Sprinkle remaining sugar over the tops (optional).
  6. Bake for 8-12 minutes in preheated oven, or until golden at the edges.
  7. Cool on wire racks, then store in airtight container at room temperature.
